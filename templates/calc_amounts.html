Breakdown of Your Personal Contributions

01

Personal Income Tax (PIT)

From your monthly gross salary, your employer transfers {{ income_tax }} GEL to the state budget. The genral PIT rate in Georgia is 20%, but it can be different or exempted from taxes based on the payment purpose.

02

Pensions Fund (PF)

If you are involved in the PF scheme, 2% of your gross salary will be transferred to the individual pension account in the pension agency by the employer. In addition, the employer and the state treasury contribute 2% of the gross salary each to the participant's pension account.

In your case, the employer transfers {{ employee_contribution }} GEL from your salary, also the employer and the state contribute to your personal PF account {{ employer_contribution }} - {{ pf_agency_contribution }} GEL respectively. Thus, the total PF contribution on your account is {{ total_pf_contribution }} GEL.

03

Value Added Tax (VAT)

Your remaining net salary is {{ net_salary }} GEL after deducting PIT and PF contributions. But it does not mean you have finished paying taxes to the State Budget. Most of the products or services you consume are taxable by VAT which is 18% in Georgia. For the exam, if you purchase chocolate and pay 5.90 GEL, the seller transfers 18% to the budget from your payment. To calculate roughly, you pay {{ vat_expenses }} GEL VAT from your net salary.
